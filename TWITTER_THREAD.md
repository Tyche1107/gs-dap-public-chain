## Thread 2: The Structural Insight (For DeFi/RWA Twitter)

🔬 Why Permissioned Blockchains Capture Large Deals, Not Large Markets

RWA case study: GS DAP vs Public Protocols

$500M (3 clients) vs $1.85B (9+ protocols)

The data reveals a pattern 🧵

---

1/ The hypothesis:

Permissioned blockchains optimize for large ticket sizes.
Public blockchains optimize for market breadth.

Both can succeed. But in different segments.

---

2/ GS DAP's sweet spot:

Average deal size: $167M

Clients:
• European Investment Bank ($100M)
• Hong Kong SAR ($750M)
• Undisclosed sovereign-level entity

Ultra-large, ultra-regulated, ultra-slow.

---

3/ Public RWA's sweet spot:

Average protocol TVL: $206M

But spread across:
• Hundreds to thousands of users
• Multiple DeFi integrations
• Composable with other protocols

---

4/ The trade-off matrix:

Permissioned (Canton):
✅ Large deals ($100M+)
✅ Regulatory comfort
✅ Brand prestige
❌ Limited network effects
❌ No DeFi composability
❌ Valuation discount (1.0x vs 2.85x)

Public (Ethereum/etc):
✅ Market breadth
✅ DeFi integration
✅ Network effects
✅ Valuation premium
❌ Smaller avg ticket
❌ Regulatory complexity

---

5/ Why GS DAP can't just "go public":

Their 3 clients specifically chose:
• Goldman brand
• Permissioned environment
• Regulatory clarity

If Canton opened → clients might leave.

If GS launched public token → different product, different clients.

---

6/ The valuation impact:

Ondo (permissionless): $1.2B on $421M TVL = 2.85x
GS DAP (permissioned): $500M on $500M TVL = 1.00x

Permissionless commands 185% premium.

Network effects matter to investors.

---

7/ Historical parallel:

Banking vs Fintech

Banks: Large clients, high touch, slow growth
Fintech: Small clients, low touch, fast growth

Both can be profitable.
But fintech gets higher multiples.

Same pattern in tokenized assets.

---

8/ The strategic question:

Should GS DAP:
(A) Stay permissioned, accept niche
(B) Launch public product, compete for breadth
(C) Do both (different brands)

Option C is hardest but most defensible long-term.

---

9/ Why this matters for RWA builders:

Don't copy GS DAP's tech.
Copy their positioning strategy.

If going permissioned → optimize for ultra-large deals
If going public → optimize for breadth + composability

Mixing strategies = falling between two stools.
